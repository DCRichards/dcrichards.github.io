Full of questions...

5 days ago January 14th, 2015

Technology is crazy, the other day they 3D printed a wrench in space. Needless to say B&Q are going to have to do well to top that. The novelty of being able to find every screw and fitting ever created in human history has nothing on NASA printing tools in space.

BREAK. How have you already digressed to DIY jokes, you've only just started blogging?

Sorry, back on topic. Basically, despite spending a number of years now doing software development, coding, computer science, bug creating or whatever title you want to give it, writing code that does stuff still continues to blow my mind a bit. And more interestingly it's both the code and the stuff that blows my mind.

TL;DR

I started this blog for various reasons, some of them in the hope of being a little more hip and cool. But aside from that, because I thought it would be cool to have this place as my sort of rubber duck. Somewhere to just throw it out there and to just keep track of interesting things I discover and maybe even create. I often find myself jokingly reasonably seriously asking what's the worst that could happen?! So let's find out, here's to giving this thing a go.

Rubber Ducks?

The title comes from the idea of rubber duck debugging. If you've ever solved a problem or fixed a bug through talking to yourself or a colleague or clueless friend without them saying a word, then you've joined the rubber duck club.
